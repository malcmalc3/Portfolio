Change Detection Map

During the summer of 2016, I was fortunate enough to be selected to participate in a National Science Foundation program called Research Experience for Undergraduates. Here, I worked with two udergraduates from different schools, with the guidance of a proffesor. By the end of the program, we were able to create a Neural Network in Matlab that was able to detect changes in two SAR (synthetic aperture radar) images of the area, but taken at different times. The reason artificial intellegence is needed for this problem is because SAR images have varying speckle noise from image to image. At the end of the program, we were able to write a paper on our process that was accepted by the 2017 ICNC Conference. The paper can be read here with an IEEE subscription.

March Madness Final Four Predictor

One of the electives I decided to take as an undergraduate was Artificial Intelligence. For one of my projects in this class, I attempted to create a Neural Network, using the R language, that could predict the outcome of the NCAA March Madness bracket. I started off with trying to predict the outcome of the Final Four from 2017, using the season averages and outcomes from the years 2012-2016. In the future, I plan to gradually expand to all 64 teams, once the network is fine tuned.
